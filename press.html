Wilted Roots
Eight artists on nature intertwining with human presence

OpenThinkingFreely announces Wilted Roots, an online presentation gathering eight artists selected for how their practices mix the natural and the human-made: Tomás Saraceno, Rune Guneriussen, Olafur Eliasson, Eduardo Kac, Brin Levinson, Caspar David Friedrich, David Yarrow, and Aletiune / Cherry. Opening online April 6, 2026, with a public program of recorded conversations and commissioned texts through June 15, 2026.

The stories that the e-flux company has to tell everyday are felt as all equally important by this online exhibitions curator, Jacob Moslander. By reading over other artists' lives and backgrounds and the works that they put all of their effort into creating today is truly motivating to read at the start of any person’s day. Motivating them to further pursue their own goals in life even if they aren’t set in a creative field. Simply being a reminder that there are so many people like others that have been in similar situations and pursuits, or in even worse environments than yourself making you feel that you are obligated to continue pushing yourself and working and keeping in mind things you take for granted others see as something to achieve for themselves that you have started with. E-flux announcements truly have the ability to open one up to so many differing forms of art and broaden their understanding of what art may be defined as, breaking down those preconceived notions and walls of what they once thought art to be defined as. Not only are these announcements a form of daily learning, but also a wonderful way of promoting artists from any background and giving them a powerful environment to have a strong and heard voice within. Both boosting their success as small artists as well as promoting the ideas, concepts, and movements that said artists feel are so important to them to include in or create art solely for. With proof of this being seen in the curator’s choice of deciding to include two artists that were previously covered by e-flux and their interviews and shown works being powerful to said curator and being first of mind to be included when they were coming up with a theme to curate an online exhibition around. With not only their personal personalities being intriguing by the vocabulary they decide to use to describe their works, but also the works themselves and how visually captivating they can be to certain people. That is why the curator of this online gallery speaks highly of e-flux and their journalism capabilities, being able to capture the feeling of works through their detailed descriptions of their physical appearances as well as the deeper meaning and significance as well as intentions the artists they interview place within the work showcased. It creates a daily announcement that is mentally nourishing and informative to be a daily read and creates a stronger connection of one’s own understanding of the art world and what occurs within it every day of the week. With our curator’s last comments on it being that they highly recommend it for any others who may feel they cannot say they have their ear to the ground in terms of the art industry’s happenings as well as those who feel the can broaden their horizons as to what art can be defined as and even find an artist they never knew would have existed that they cannot live without now.
