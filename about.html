About the exhibition

Why Wilted Roots

The reason as to why I called this exhibition, Wilted Roots, is because I wanted the theme of this collection of artists and their pieces to be about the collaboration between nature and the unnatural that humans bring with them. How the two don’t have to be opposites or have no correlation with one another, that they can come together to create something that couldn’t be possible without the other. As well as show that and be able to understand that message within the works themselves. With the use of describing a man made object of a road, whether it be made of cement taken from the earth and formed with human hands to thus create an unnatural object, or a dirt road in the grass that also would only be formed by heavy footfall repeatedly that only occurs with large groups of people, with wording reserved for natural objects such as wilting. Or to bloom, words that they themselves hold much meaning that I feel is connected with a more natural side of things. Thus combining the two to show how the two are not separate entities, that they rely on each other in a multitude of ways unexplainable in a little amount of time.

Why this theme centers the exhibit

As to why I specifically chose this theme to center the exhibit around, it is because it’s something that I feel specifically interested in. I like to think of how interconnected everything is truly and how everything you feel and see is connected to both the earth that we inhabit and the people that graze it as well. That is sort of what I want viewers to understand when seeing the artists’ works, I want them to feel that connection with the earth, how they are always connected with their feet to the ground of this rock in space and truly be grateful for the things that may seem to be taken for granted such as that floor that we utilize everyday, or the trees we walk under to protect ourselves from other gifts given to us like the sun. Really bring home the point of needing to slow down and just see how everything in our lives is connected in some way and not overlook it and give it the time of day I feel it truly deserves.

What I want viewers to think about

I want viewers to really think, where does my body end and where does “nature” begin? With there being no real answer to it other than what you feel is right. One may feel it ends where their personal body ends, with the rest being natural forces that affect oneself, or there may be no end to it, that a person’s body is just another branch of nature and what it creates and thus everything and everyone is connected. The answer is up to the individual and all conclusions are equal in terms of correctness when it comes to me.

What I want viewers to feel

Emotionally, I want the exhibition to feel serene. Calm in hopes of not getting in the way of a viewer's inner thoughts and workings towards the questions I want them to find answers to. Feeling somewhat desolate, but not in a hopeless sort of way, but rather an optimistic and tranquil one. Moreso, the remnants of human existence can be found here, which means that starting again is possible rather than it all has collapsed and failed.

The exhibition online

While this exhibition is solely online which I feel may make the theme of interconnectedness a bit more difficult to grasp, I still feel that it is doable. Seeing as the internet is an entirely human made idea and object, the uses of it are also completely natural. With social media mimicking physical community spaces like waterholes for ecosystems of animals in the wild. Being millions of digital waterholes for specific groups of people to find themselves gathering to find others like them. So with this idea of digital ecosystems being similar to those we see in the world we inhabit, I hope the themes of community, togetherness, and nature being intertwined with humans and unnatural creations like “websites” can come across and will be able to show how other artists see these connections between the two as well.

These eight artists

The reason as to why I decided on these eight artists was because I felt they had artwork that truly got across the theme I wanted to work with and also was personally just really interesting work just on its own merit. I hope the curation I chose does well in its intention to bring about that feeling of connection and mixing of natural and unnatural worlds.

Tomás Saraceno

I chose Tomás because of the way in which he describes his works, likening them to the actions of animals in nature, yet are completely manmade in appearance. It is the explanation behind the work and the meaning it is trying to convey which makes that connection of nature and humans. Replicating nature in a more metaphorical way rather than a more average approach of using primarily visuals so that a viewer of his works is required to look more closely and really think about what it is they are looking at.

Rune Guneriussen

I felt that Rune’s works were truly eye-catching. While I feel that at first look they seem rather simple, I feel that the way in which the objects are placed and how the images are captured really give the final work strong emotions and feelings. Playing with the man made objects and the natural resources they are derived from, putting them back into their habitats in odd and peculiar ways.

Olafur Eliasson

The way in which Olafur plays with light and creates abstract viewing through it is truly interesting. He is able to make completely different feelings all from using the same primary idea of using light in some way in his works. With the intertwining of nature and man-made objects through the contraptions Eliasson creates to capture light and cast it in new and different ways not seen before.

Eduardo Kac

Eduardo is very different from the rest of this list, being more so on the technological, or man-made side of the theme and shows how nature is integrated with humans and art through a more scientific lens. Utilizing science and technology to create biological works or even beings not before possible in the natural world without human intervention.

Brin Levinson

On the opposite side of the spectrum to Eduardo, Levinson depicts this connection of nature and humanity as that nature will always thrive in the end. Showing that the world that we see as normal being long forgotten and now simply more land for the animals here long before us, still being around long after us. I feel as if his works are to retaliate against the idea of the fear people have of animals going extinct because of human existence. That they simply are gone for the moment, but there can become a time in which they could return, once there is a fear that humans could become extinct.

Caspar David Friedrich

The main reason as to why I chose Friedrich was because of the fact that his works are what I deem to be classics in terms of paintings. But I also felt they fit the theme when you look at the content of the actual paintings. With a good amount showing a deep connection humans have with the natural world even in a time of the past.

David Yarrow

I felt Yarrow’s works to be a very good depiction of the theme based on the content in which David photographs. With his work primarily focusing on the connection between man and animal. Showing both animals in human environments, and vice versa. Showing that the two can coexist and support each other.

Aletiune / Cherry

This is a selection of mine from an artist that I personally follow online. I feel their work is a good amalgamation of cold metal and futuristic steel bent in alien ways, with the cropping and abstract photos of greenery and the natural world that feels more understandable and warm by what surrounds it in most of their works. This being amplified by the start white that often surrounds the entire work so that your eye is guided towards the familiar imagery to then lead to the unfamiliar.
